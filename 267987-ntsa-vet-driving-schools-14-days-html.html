NTSA to vet all driving schools within 14 days

- The National Transport and Safety Authority(NTSA) said all driving schools must be vetted to deal with increased cases of accidents caused by incompetent drivers

- The mandatory scrutiny will take place in the next 14 days beginning Wednesday,March 7

- Driving schools are required to download application forms from the safety authority's portal,fill them and submit them to nearest NTSA offices

- Institutions which will fail to be vetted will have their operating licences cancelled

- NTSA is also vetting all PSV operators and Saccos to weed out rogue ones

All driving schools have until Wednesday, March 21 to know whether they will continue operating or risk having their license canceled.

Transport regulator National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) on Wednesday March 7, announced plans to vet all driving schools in an effort to curb road carnage that continue to wreck havoc on Kenyan roads.

"The objective of this exercise is to address the current high rate of road traffic crashes attributed to incompetency of drivers," said the regulator.

"The exercise is further expected to weed out the driving schools operating without the set requirements," added NTSA in a statement seen by TUKO.co.ke.

The mandatory exercise which will be conducted by a task force comprising NTSA, Ministry of Interior as well as Ministry of Transport and Infrastructure begins immediately and will take place withing the next 14 days.

The road watchdog also urged the said institutions to download vetting forms from the safety authority's official website and submit them to nearest NTSA offices within the specified period.

Already, the task force has launched a two-week vetting of Passenger Service Vehicles (PSVs).

As a result, president Uhuru Kenyatta ordered the NTSA officers to be removed from highways.
