Police Service Deny Officer Was Snatched Phone by Thieves, Says Viral Video Was Staged

The National Police Service (NPS) has dismissed the viral video circulating on social media showing an officer being snatched a phone by suspected thieves.

In the video, the unidentified police officer is thought to have fallen victim to marauding Nairobi thugs after a thief on a motorbike snatched a mobile phone from him.

Taking to Twitter on Wednesday, July 7, the Hillary Mutyambai led service insisted that the video was manipulated to taint the good reputation of the disciplined force.

"Our investigations have revealed that a video circulating on social media insinuating to be of a police officer being snatched a phone is a manipulated video whose intention is malicious. We urge members of the public to ignore it," read the Tweet.

The police officer was manning traffic at the Roysambu roundabout on Tuesday, July 6, when the incident happened.

A viral video taken from a dashboard camera by a motorist showed the incident occurred at around 5pm.

In the footage, two boda boda riders could be seen riding past the officer making a phone call; one rode past behind him before another boda boda that was carrying two passengers, approached.

One of the passengers daringly grabbed the officer's phone leaving him confused as the motorbike sped off to an unknown destination.
