Earnings through music

D*ggystyle, the rapper's debut album was produced by Dr Dre's Death Row Records and released in 1993. It debuted at the peak of both the B Billboard Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums and Billboard 200 charts. This album sold 806,858 copies and raked in over $6 million on the first week alone. It contains several hit singles like Gin and Juice and What's My Name.

In 1996, the rapper released his next album, dubbed Doggfather. Just like the previous one, this production also debuted at the top position on both Billboard charts. Snoop's subsequent three albums were produced at No Limit Records.

This includes Da Game Is to Be Sold in 1998, Not to Be Told a year later, No Limit Top Dogg in 1999, and Tha Last Meal in 2000. He then left for EMI Records in 2002, where he released his album Paid the Cost to Be da Boss.
