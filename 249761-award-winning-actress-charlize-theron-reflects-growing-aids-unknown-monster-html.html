Award-winning actress Charlize Theron reflects on growing up when Aids was an unknown 'monster'

- Theron was visiting her native home to initiate a program seeking to help adolescent girls get information about sexual health

- She shared her experience as child in South Africa when individuals had remote knowledge about HIV/Aids

- More than five million South Africans are living with the virus

- About 3 million of them are said to be on treatment

Hollywood actress Charlize Theron has explained her prospects to create an AIDS-free society back in her native country, South Africa.

The 42-year old star who is also the founder of non-governmental organization supporting community-based programs uncovered that South Africa's strangely substantial HIV load was difficult to overlook.

She said she was glad of South Africa's measures to curb the spread of the virus, albeit many individuals who ought to be on treatment are still not getting it.

Theron, who left South Africa as a youngster asserted that sex inequality and destitution assumed a noteworthy part in the spread of HIV.

She sparked controversy in 2016 when she contended that the virus which is consuming the continent was accelerated by sexism, destitution and homophobia.

During her visit, she interacted with young girls in Soweto, one of South Africa's neighborhood scourged by joblessness and poverty.

Some of the teenagers are AIDS orphans and often exposed to crime and sexual violence, making them vulnerable to the virus.
