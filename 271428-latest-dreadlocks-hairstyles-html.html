40+ Latest dreadlocks hairstyles for men and women in 2022

One of the most intriguing things about hair is its ability to say so much about someone's personality based on how they choose to wear it. Some bold, head-turning hairstyles can communicate more about you than words ever could. The dreadlock hairstyle is one of the most interesting ones with its rich heritage, bold looks, and numerous designs. What are the latest dreadlocks hairstyles one can try today?

Modern-day dreadlocks hairstyles present numerous possibilities from super short to extra-long and everything in between.

So, which are the best dreadlocks hairstyles? Here is a look at the best dreadlocks styles for ladies and men.

1. Low ponytail

The low ponytail dreadlocks look works particularly well for black men. If you work in a somewhat conservative setting that allows one to wear dreads, the low ponytail is inarguably one of the most elegant ways to style them.

2. Fade with dreads

The classic fade has been a staple of male fashion for decades. Men with dreadlocks can incorporate various fades to give the locs an even better look. Feel free to try the different types of fades (low, mid, or high) and see which one works best with your dreads.

The low tapered fade results in a clean, professional look. The mid or temp fade is the perfect balance between low and high fade and bold and classy. If you feel bold enough with your locs, try pairing them with a high fade cut.

3. Bold mohawk

The mohawk is arguably the boldest dreads men's style today. It is no surprise that there are numerous ways to wear your locs in a mohawk. The resulting look is quite cool and can be created with a tapered fade on the sides. Additionally, you can try the look with undercut or shaved sides.

4. Undercut dreadlocks

To emphasize your undercut dread locks hairstyle, try pairing your locs with an undercut. The undercut creates short hair on the sides, cut to a uniform length all around. The resulting stylish and handsome look is one of the best dread styles for guys right now.

Short dreadlocks on top of the head keep the look classic and tight. Pairing the undercut with longer hair creates a fashionable look that is super easy to pull off. Undercut locs look great with black hair.

5. Curly locs

You can curl your locs for a bold and stylish look. You can try various curl lengths to create varying looks and textures. The curly locs style is also one of the best boy dreadlocks styles.

6. Crisscross dreads

Crisscross dreads can appeal to beginners whose locs are still quite short and cannot be styled into other designs. Still, the crisscross design also looks amazing on people with long locs. So feel free to let the lower half of the locs hang freely.

7. Short locs

While many people envision having long dreads, short locs are more versatile and easier to style. Beginners can start with low-maintenance styles, such as those with tapered or undercut sides.

8. Bro flow

The bro flow is among the best long dread hairstyles for guys looking for an effortless and relaxed look. The flowing long locs are swept away from the face and then cut to shoulder length. Keep in mind that only those with significantly long dreads can wear this style. Feel free to let the hair strands flow or tie them up in a ponytail.

9. Braided dreadlocks

Braided dreadlocks are somewhat similar to cornrows. However, cornrows are created using small strands of hair gathered on the scalp. Braided locs are achieved using thicker dreadlocked hair braided all over the head or on the scalp, depending on one's preference.

10. Hippie dreadlocks

This style is ideal for those looking to give their hair some time to relax and move freely. Simply put, this style lets your locks flow freely without the tension on the hair often associated with gathering hair into buns, ponytails, or pigtails.

11. Grey dreadlocks

The fact that grey locs look amazing highlights how amazing the hairstyle works for men of every age. As a growing fashion trend, more and more older people can be seen spotting grey locs that only seem to get cooler as the years go by.

12. Dreadlocked rows

Rows can be braided or twisted into one's scalp regardless of the thickness of the locs. The dreadlocked rows style is considerably tricky to pull off but is definitely one of the most stylish ones. Again, consult your hairstylist on the best way to achieve the look.

13. Dyed dreads

This is the ultimate way to wear dreads for bold people. Dyed locs give one the option to incorporate various colours into their hairstyle. The dyed look can be fascinating with a combination of colours such as red, white, and blonde. However, be careful when using dyes. Some can damage your scalp and hair strands.

14. Basketweave dreadlocks

As the name suggests, this style resembles the strands on a woven basket. Basketweave dreadlocks work with hair of any length but are best applied on long locs. You will want to seek out a skilled hairstylist's services for the best outcome.

15. Short ombre locks

The ombre shading technique is among today's trendiest dreadlocks hairstyles. Feel free to try the ombre effect in a gradual shift from black at the roots to blond at the tips. The resulting trendy look explains why the ombre look is all the rage right now.

16. Black bun with a lineup

This statement updo is the perfect combination of stylish, neat, and classy. The lineup is the key element of this style and makes the style quite the head-turner.

17. Chunky Locs

Maintaining a chunky loc appearance is not easy. But, as one of the most popular dreadlock styles for men, it is inarguably a winner. These locs have a rugged look to them, making this one of the best ways to wear dreadlocks for men with a full beard.

18. Two-tone locs

Two-tone colours on locs are very cool, particularly the vintage colours. The tone adds edge, class, and a nice depth to the hair. Feel free to try any two complementary colours to create your tone.

19. Free boho locs

For a chunkier take on the hippie style, try out some free-flowing boho locs. Typically, this style requires quite some patience for it to appear, but it is well worth the wait. Feel free to use some cool grips to give extra flair to your locs.

20. Neat skinny dreadlocks

When beginning your dreadlocks journey, you can choose to grow them thick, thin, or medium. You can also choose to let them flow, braid them, or tie them into a ponytail or bun. The skinny dreadlocks style is ideal for clean, manageable locs. The look is particularly appealing when paired with a connecting beard.

21. Fire flame locs

If you are going for the unmistakable 'wow factor' in your locs, you cannot go wrong with the fire flame locs. With clean, shaved sides and bold colours on the locs, this style will definitely have people turning their heads everywhere you go.

Dreads styles for women

Are you trying to figure out the ideal dreadlocks style for a woman? Here are some amazing options to consider.

22. Twisted dreadlocks

Twisted dreads allow one to experiment with numerous textures, looks, and styles. The twisted locs styles can be gathered over one shoulder for a timeless and elegant look.

23. Dreadlocked halo

This style can be achieved using a combination of thin and thick strands of locs. The crown on top of the head is the key aspect of this style. You can also add some highlights and accessories to give the halo more flair.

24. Wool dreads

Wrapping locs with wool began as a protective measure designed to prevent frizzing. However, with time, the wool dreads became a fashion statement. You can try different wool colours for various outcomes. Some of the most common include white, black, brown, and blond. Keep in mind that wool dreads become extremely heavy when wet and often take longer to dry.

25. Crinkle dreads

Crinkle locs are a quirky and fun twist to the conventional dreadlocks style. The process to get crinkle dreads is similar to that of wavy locs. Begin by making your locs wet, then plait them into three huge braids. Tuck the braids into a hairband and leave the hair to dry. Afterwards, unbraid the locs to reveal the nice-looking crinkles.

26. Curly locs

Are you looking for the perfect short dread styles for women? You should consider trying curly locs. This style can be achieved by parting the dreads in the middle and then shaping them like curls. The look closely resembles that of the famous Medusa.

27. Mohawk with loopy dreadlocks

This style combines kinky dreads layered in two heaps, undercut hair, and flat twists. You can also accessorize it with fancy earrings, beads, or cowries to enhance the look. This is one of those locs styles suitable for bold women.

28. Wavy dreads

Wavy dreadlocks are a simple way to change up your style with a playful and romantic result. You can make your dreadlocks wavy using satin-covered or silk hair rollers. Roll and clip them as you would with regular hair, apply setting lotion to damp dreads, and then loosely braid them. Leave the locs to dry for the perfect waves.

29. Rasta dreads

Dreadlocks have been associated with Rastafarianism for quite a long time, and rightly so. Well, the Rasta dreads hairdo is among the most common among Rastafarians. It is also among the elegantly simple dreadlocks hairstyles ever. The style is achieved by letting dreads grow freely without using any unnatural techniques to create, rub, roll, or style them. However, they can still be washed like other locs.

30. Dreadlocks fishtail

The dreads fishtail is perhaps one of the most artistic ways to wear your locs. Having dreadlocked hair does not mean you have to give upbraiding it. In fact, locs are way easier to braid than regular hair. To get the fishtail look, divide your dreads into thick sections and braid them to form a fishtail-looking design.

31. Two-coloured dreads

If you are worried that your hair might not have sufficient volume to form locs, you could opt for dreads extensions. This will give you the option to experiment with different colours, one on your natural hair and another on the extension. For example, you can opt for natural black dreads with grey extensions.

32. High loopy bun

For most women who wear dreadlocks, puling the locs into a bun is often the easiest way to style them. Take this method a notch higher by styling the locs up into a loopy bun. The resulting look can be worn in both formal and informal settings.

33. Dreads with beads

Very few hairstyles scream fun as beaded dreads do. Feel free to wear your beaded locs down, parted to the sides, or up. Opt for beads in different bright colours for an even more enhanced look.

Beads can be used to accessorize numerous beginner short dreadlocks styles for ladies.

34. Dread pin-up

Dread pin-ups are the pinnacle of the versatility associated with dreadlocks. Try to create a timeless look by twisting some of your locs around a foam roller then securing them with bobby pins. If your hair has braided sides, the pin-up look will look even more elegant.

35. Faux locs

Faux locs are also known as 'fake dreads.' If you love how dreadlocks look but are not willing to invest the time required to grow female natural dreadlocks, faux locs are the way to go. You can achieve the look by having your stylist braid your real hair before wrapping the faux locs around it. The effect can also be achieved using cornrows or crotchets.

36. Wavy dreads

Wavy dreads have a relaxed vibe to them. Try this style to bring out your playful side. One can also take this style a notch higher by cutting off the locs in the front to create the timeless bangs look synonymous with the 90s fashion.

37. Sea green dreadlocks

This is the perfect way to wear mermaid style dreads. Colour the hair with a teal shade on the roots and gradually blend it into a sea-green hue towards the middle. The result is a head-turning sea-green look that you can be sure is not worn by many people.

38. Space buns

This style is among the most popular, even using other hair types. Dreadlocked space buns can be quite exciting to try. The parted dreads create a super neat look, while the buns are effortlessly pretty. If you feel bold enough with your dreads, feel free to give this one a try.

39. Tower hairstyle

If you have a flair for the dramatic, then the tower hairstyle is just the one for you. However, keep in mind that the dreads tower hairstyle is among the trickiest to pull off. Still, you will be incredibly proud of the result if you manage to do it.

40. Long blonde dreads

You can get creative with blonde dreads. Try braiding them, twisting, swirling, or even adding some accessories. The length and colour of these dreads make them perfect for experimentation.

41. The doughnut bun

You can use your locks to create a perfect bun on top of your head. Besides looking amazing, this style also takes the hair away from your neck, which is perfect for hot weather. Feel free to accessorize the doughnut using a headband for that tropical vibe.
