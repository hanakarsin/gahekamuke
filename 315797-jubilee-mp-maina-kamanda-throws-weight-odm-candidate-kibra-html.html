Jubilee MP Maina Kamanda throws weight behind ODM candidate for Kibra

- Kamanda said he decision is informed by the spirit of handshake initiated between President Uhuru Kenyatta and Raila Odinga

- His move came barely a day after Uhuru endorsed MacDonald Mariga as the Jubilee nominee for the Kibra by-election

- The lawmaker stated the decision was personal and he did not speak for any other person in the ruling the party

Jubilee's nominated MP Maina Kamanda has endorsed the candidature of Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) nominee for Kibra seat.

Kamanda who was addressing the press at Raila Odinga' Capitol Hill office said he will support Imran Okoth to replace his late brother Ken Okoth.

The vocal lawmaker said his decision to support ODM in Kibra was personal and was informed by the spirit of handshake between President Uhuru Kenyatta and Raila.

" I will support the ODM candidate in the Kibra by-election because to me his like the Jubilee candidate. This is my personal decision and it should be clear that I am not speaking for anyone," he said

Kamanda stated he holds the former premier in high regards and appreciates his commitment to foster peace and unity in the country and deemed it important to support the ODM candidate for the Kibra contest.

"The Handshake brought us harmony and peace in the country.. As Jubilee we have a candidate but as a person, I will be with the ODM candidate. I am here to stand with the candidate nominated by ODM Imran Okoth," he added

His decision to back Imran came barely a day after the Uhuru endorsed McDonald Mariga to fly the ruling party's flag in the simmering city contest.

The by-election has brought to the fore political tussles and shenanigans that has seen former allies part ways in been described by observers as a classical game of betrayal.

While many expected Amani National Congress (ANC) and FORD Kenya to support Raila's outfit considering they were affiliate parties under the National Super Alliance (NASA), two parties opted to field candidates for the city seat, complicating ODM's numerical strength.

The contest got murkier on Wednesday after the president openly endorsed Mariga to battle out with other candidates contrary to expectations of many.

A section of Kenyans expected that Uhuru would abstain from taking sides in Kibra by-election in honour of the handshake deal with the ODM leader.

Raila's allies have already read malice in the president's move and have expressed their displeasure accusing him of betrayal.

Embakasi East MP Babu Owino and Suba South lawmaker Millie Odhiambo accused Jubilee Party leadership of going against the spirit of the handshake and attempting to elbow them out even in their own home turf.
