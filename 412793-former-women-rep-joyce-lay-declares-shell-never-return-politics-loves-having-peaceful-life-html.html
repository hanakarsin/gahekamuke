Former Women Rep Joyce Lay Declares She'll Never Return to Politics: &quot;I'm Not Looking Back&quot;

- Joyce Lay opened up about her journey since quitting politics

- According to the former women representative, people really hurt her so bad that she is not planning on taking part in next year's General Election

- Her followers encouraged her to keep on choosing herself and finding peace

Former Taita Taveta women representative Joyce Lay has confirmed that she will not be going back into politics anytime soon.

Lay shared a long post on Instagram answering followers who constantly questioned her on whether she will be making a comeback to the world of politics.

Part of what she shared online read:

"For those who are asking, I am at a different stage of my life separate from politics. This is where I am happy most. So I want to hang around for a while and just enjoy the tranquillity. It is beautiful up here. It looks like an ocean of clouds and I am all alone. Yet so peaceful. I found my purpose and I have just started walking towards it. I am not looking back."

According to the gorgeous politician turned gospel artiste, she will not be vying for any seat in Taita Taveta county in the next year's General Election.

"Even the deputy governor positions that were offered to me, I will not take any. It's too early for me to go back now. It was too much on me. I lost everything. I had so much pain. I was shattered. Broken, wounded, broke the whole time and the people I trusted turned against me,'' she added.
