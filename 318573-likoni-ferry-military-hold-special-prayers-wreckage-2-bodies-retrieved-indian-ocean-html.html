Likoni ferry: Military to hold special prayers after car, 2 bodies retrieved from Indian Ocean

- Relatives and friends gathered at the shore to view bodies of Amanda and her mother Kighenda

- The two drowned when their car fell off the ferry on Sunday, September 29

- Government announced the military would conduct a special service before the bodies are handed over to the family

A sombre mood has engulfed the shores of Indian Ocean as Kenyans await the retrieval of the ill-fated car carrying bodies of two victims who drowned on Sunday, September 29.

Relatives, friends and the public have camped at the Likoni ferry to see bodies of Mariam Kighenda and daughter Amanda arrive on the shore later on Friday, October 11.

The government spokesperson Cyrus Oguna announced the military would conduct a special service before the bodies are handed over to the family.

The family will decide where the bodies will be taken.

Oguna detailed the procedure to be followed after the wreckage and bodies are retrieved and added the family of the victims will be allowed to conduct a short prayer service at the scene.

The retrieval mission was, however, hampered by a light shower that lasted few minutes after 3pm.

Though only two bodies were expected out of the wreckage, the Kenya Red Cross volunteers came with three stretchers to carry remains of Mariam and baby Amanda.

The government had on Wednesday, October 9, assured family the recovery exercise will be brought to a close on Thursday, but that was not the case due to heavy currents at the ocean.

Oguna also confirmed there were bodies inside the vehicle and rescuers were cautious to have them out of the water together with the the car.

"We have equipment to remove the cars but the currents are strong and making it difficult to hook the entire car and pull it out. We need to have the car and the bodies. We hope by tomorrow (Friday), we will have found ways to achieve that. Our fear is to pull the car and lose it halfway because we may lose the bodies and end up doing zero work," he said.

The government spokesman also said the team has been in close communication with the bereaved family.
