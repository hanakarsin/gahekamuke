- According to Kuria, the project was long overdue since residents in the area have been using open-air toilets

Murang'a senator Irungu Kang'ata has accused police of what he termed as blocking Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria and Juja parliamentary aspirant George Koimburi from opening a public washroom in Juja.

Koimburi is flying Kuria's Peoples Empowerment Party (PPE) party flag in the race.

According to Kang'ata, the move by the police to block the duo from launching the project amounted to a breach of constitutional rights of assembly and movement.

"Police blocking George Koimburi and Moses Kuria in Juja from opening a self-funded public washroom. This breaches the Constitutional rights of assembly and movement. When shall we mature as a democratic country?" posed Kang'ata.

According to Kuria, the project was long overdue since residents have been using open-air toilets in land belonging to some very wealthy families to relieve themselves.

"For the many, men, women and children at Witeithie Market have been using open-air toilets in land belonging very wealthy families. That is why George Koimburi and myself decided to construct a modern block of toilets for them," Kuria said.

The toilets were also repleted with several bathrooms.

Kuria accused the government of failing to provide the residents with the facilities.

"The government has failed to provide even a basic toilet for them. When we tried to launch the block of toilets today, Friday, May 7, police sought for reinforcements from as far as Mwingi to stop two people," Kuria posted on his Facebook account.

Kuria said that the residents of Witeithie in Juja will now have an opportunity to use the clean toilet as those used by their tormentors.

"You may have stopped us, but verily I say unto you Pharaoh, my people of Witeithie will from today use a toilet as clean as that used by your wife because they are also human beings," Kuria said.

On Tuesday, May 4, Kuria claimed that the government had banned him from being in any part of Juja.

"I am tired of always having to wear a Buibui every time I am in Juja to avoid arrest," said Kuria.

Kuria had expressed confidence that Koimburi, who is perceived as one of the strongest candidates in the coming by-election, will win the polls.

Koimburi ditched the ruling Jubilee Party and joined Kuria's PPE.

The Juja parliamentary seat fell vacant following the death of former MP Francis Waitutu alias Wakapee.
