A security guard manning Cubes exhibition stores in Nairobi has died after a stray bullet hit him.

The shooting incident happened when two suspected thugs accosted seasoned Kenyan actor Ainea Ojiambo along Moi Avenue in Nairobi's central business district on Friday, April 30.

A CCTV footage seen by TUKO.co.ke showed Ojiambo talking to an unidentified woman before two criminals approached, went straight to him and started wrestling him.

The two assailants unsuccessfully tried to snatch Ojiambo's gold neck chain, prompting him to draw his pistol, scaring away his attackers who immediately took to their heels.

In the ensuing melee, two gunshots rented the air. A security officer was allegedly shot in the head, and another bullet hit a passer-by.

Police took the body of the security guard to City Mortuary awaiting postmortem while the passer-by was rushed to the hospital for emergency treatment.

The suspect was arrested and driven to Central Police Station to record statement.

The incident elicited mixed reactions from a section of Kenyans who raised concerns about the level of insecurity in the city centre.

"Nairobi CBD has become very dangerous for any person walking around. Thugs are robbing others in broad daylight, sometimes with knives. COVID-19 has made people poorer and move vicious and daring. That Moi Avenue incident was a terrible one. The guard didn't have to die," commented Juma G.
