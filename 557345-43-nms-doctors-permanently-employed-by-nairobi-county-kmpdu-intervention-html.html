43 NMS Doctors Permanently Employed by Nairobi County after KMPDU Intervention

  • Kenya Medical Practitioners, Pharmacists, and Dentists' Union secretary-general Davji Atellah said the Nairobi county government would confirm medical officers who worked under NMS
  • Atellah said Governor Johnson Sakaja committed to have all 43 doctors contracted by NMS transit to permanent and pensionable terms
  • The KMPDU boss thanked the county government for its cooperation and commitment to recognising the important role played by doctors

Amos Khaemba, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings over three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

Nairobi - Governor Johnson Sakaja has agreed to absorb doctors who were previously contracted by the Nairobi Metropolitan Service (NMS).

On Thursday, August 1, Kenya Medical Practitioners, Pharmacists, and Dentists' Union (KMPDU) secretary general Davji Atellah disclosed that Nairobi county would confirm 115 medical officers on permanent and pensionable terms.

When will NMS doctors pick up their letters?

Atellah said that after the union's meeting with Sakaja, it was agreed that the affected medics would pick up their letters of employment on August 12, 2024.

"In our agreement, all 43 doctors (35 MOs & 8 specialists) will be confirmed to permanent and pensionable terms. Doctors will pick up their letters on 12th August 2024.This means all 115 medical officers and 18 specialists under NMS will be confirmed to permanent and pensionable terms, with additional employment of 35 MOs, 31 pharmacists, 20 dentists, and 8 specialists," Atellah said.

The KMPDU boss thanked the county government for its cooperation and commitment to recognising the important role played by doctors.

"We sincerely thank Governor Sakaja Johnson for his cooperation and commitment to recognising the vital contributions of our medical professionals. At KMPDU we are committed to ensuring no member is left behind. We are stronger together," he added.

What KMPDU agreed with the government about interns

In other news, over 1,200 interns will be posted after KMPDU signed a deal with the government.

Mary Muthoni, the Principal Secretary (PS) for Public Health and Professional Standards in the Ministry of Health Kenya, represented the government.

Atellah said the agreement would render useless the previous offer, which would see the interns take home a KSh 70,000 monthly stipend.

On the remuneration, KMPDU said the Eldoret Employment and Labour Relations Court would determine the matter.
