About Kenya High School

Kenya High is located in Kileleshwa, a posh Nairobian suburb ranks among the top national public girls' schools. The learning institution was formerly known as Nairobi European School and was established in 1910. It later became a girl’s school in 1932.

The segregation of learners saw it called European Girls Secondary School, with Miss Kerby as the school's first head in 1935. The learning institution was renamed Kenya High School and was later acquired by the Kenyan government in 1974. The change in management allowed the school to admit more African pupils.

Also, the school got its first headmistress, Mrs. R. Kariuki, in 1977, taking over from Miss Barnes. However, she only headed the learning centre for a few months leaving in July of the same year, handing it over to M. W. Wanjohi.

With the surging enrollment rate, Kenya High School fees became competitive compared to other secondary schools of its calibre.

Students in the learning institution increased over time. In the late 1930s, Kenya High had only 35 students. The number increased in the 40s to 150 and then 500 in the 50s. Today, the well-performing school attracts students from different parts of the country and has a population of more than 1,300 students.

Kenya High has been a top performer among national schools in Kenya for several decades. The institution has over ten dormitories, named after legendary European women, each with a capacity of 130 students.

Kenya High School admission requirements

What are the Kenya High School entry marks? The outspoken high school with a rich history is a national school, which means that entry is regulated by the government's directive on the cut off points for national schools in Kenya. Also, it is essential to note that these cut off points alter every year based on performance and subscription.

Regardless of the government's directive on the cut off points, if you want to be on the safe side, score 400 marks and above in your KCPE.

However, it is still unclear how students under the new CBC system will transition to the learning institution considering the structures are quite different from 8-4-4. Students from marginalized areas can join Kenya High School with 320 marks and above.

Kenya High enrolment procedure

Students earn slots via the nationwide selection process. Once the process is done, learners can download their admission letters via the ministry's online portal.

If you had applied to the school, used the appropriate KNEC code and attained the right marks, you are sure to get an admission here.

The admission letter will have all the details of the school required when reporting. All you need to do to access your letter is enter your index number on the official portal for form one selections, choose your county and sub-county, and then click submit.

Take your admission letter to your primary school’s headteacher for endorsement. They will stamp it on the spaces provided.

After getting your letter certified, present it with a copy of your birth certificate to Kenya High School on the date specified for admission.

Kenya High School dorms and student life

The famous national high school has around 1,300 students in ten dorms every year. These dorms house about 130 students each.

The dorms have different names, including, Tausi (formerly Huxley) & Sabaki (Cubbie), Chania (Beale) & Yala (Nightingale), Nyali (Mortimer), Suswa (Mitchell), Sagana (Hamilton) & Naivasha (Bronte), Baden Powell (has retained its name, but is often referred to by its acronym, BP) & Mara (Curie)

The Kenya High School fees

Kenya High School fee structure comprises both government-sponsored funds and parent contributions. As a public high school, Kenya High has to comply with the Kenya secondary school fees structure unveiled by the Ministry of Education.

Since Kenya High is a boarding secondary school, the government provides capitation for each learner equal to capitation for every learner in a day school.

Also, parents are meant to pay the boarding fees applicable to the school to meet the cost of boarding. And this boarding fee varies for different categories of schools.

The school’s total payable fee is KShs. 67,244. Parents pay a larger share of KSh. 45,000 while the government pays KShs. 22,244 per student.

Kenya High School uniform

All public secondary schools in Kenya have uniforms, and Kenya High is no different. The students wear grey skirts, white blouses, and grey ties. Also, the secondary school is renowned for its dominant brown shoes, quite different from what other secondary schools prefer (black).
