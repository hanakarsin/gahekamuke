Dubai-Based Aleph Hospitality Takes over Management of Kenya's Three Boma Inn Hotels

- Three Boma hotels will no longer be under the Red Court Hotel

- The Aleph Hospitality will be looking forward to turning around the fortunes of the hotels

- Profits earned in the cause will be channelled towards charity

Dubai's Aleph Hospitality Company has taken over the management of Kenya's Boma hotels after an agreement with Kenya Red Cross.

Before the takeover, the hotels, Boma Nairobi, Boma Inn Nairobi and Boma Inn Eldoret were under the Red Court Hotel which is associated with the Kenya Red Cross Society.

Aleph Hospitality will be looking forward to revamping the Boma brand which had been somehow troubled by among others, the COVID-19 pandemic which has had adverse effects on the hospitality industry.

The agreement entails the Dubai-based agency offering financial support to Kenya Red Cross from the profits earned from the three hotels.

The management of Aleph is upbeat that their partnership with the Kenya Red Cross will be worthwhile thus recovering the fortunes of the Boma hotels.

"We are delighted to take over the management of the three Boma hotels in a deal that further strengthens our presence in Kenya. We are committed to working with the owners to turn around the fortunes of these wonderful hotels and proud to support local humanitarian projects being run by the owner through the Kenya Red Cross,‚Äù said Bani Haddad, Founder and Managing Director of Aleph Hospitality.

On his part, former Kenya Red Cross secretary-general Dr Abbas Gullet expressed his joy stating Aleph Hospitality was fit enough to turn around the business in the hotels.

He further added the profits made in the cause will be instrumental in funding charitable initiatives.

"We are thrilled to be working with a professional hotel management company that understands both the global hotel business and our local hospitality market. All profits from Boma Nairobi, Boma Inn Nairobi and Boma Inn Eldoret fund the Kenya Red Cross Society and we are confident that together we can re-establish successful hotel businesses that will help support this great cause," he said.

Boma Nairobi borders both Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) and Wilson Airport.

The hotel features 148 rooms and suites, a conference hall, boardroom, two food and beverage outlets, recreational facilities including a gym, spa and swimming pool.

Boma Inn Nairobi is based at the Kenya Red Cross complex, still near to the JKIA and Wilson Airport. It has 58 rooms, an all-day-dining restaurant and a lounge.

The remaining, Boma Inn Eldoret, is found in the Eldoret town, some kilometres from the Eldoret International Airport. It has 68 rooms and suites, three food and beverage outlets, conference facilities, a gym, a spa and a swimming pool.

Besides the Boma hotels, Aleph Hospitality also runs other projects in the country including the Best Western Plus which found in Westlands, Nairobi.

The agency also operates the Kisumu-based Bluewater Hotel under the guise of Protea Hotels by Marriott.

Established in 2015, Aleph Hospitality operates hotels on behalf of owners either on a franchise basis for branded properties or as a white-label operator for independent hotels.
