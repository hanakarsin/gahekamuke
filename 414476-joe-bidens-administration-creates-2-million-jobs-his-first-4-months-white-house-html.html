Joe Biden's Administration Creates 2 Million Jobs in His First 4 Months at White House

- President Joe Biden's administration has created over 2 million jobs since he took office in January 2021

- This is the most jobs created in the first four months of any presidency since records began in 1939

-According to the head of state, long-term unemployment also dropped by the second-largest amount ever recorded

The United States of America (USA) President Joe Biden has announced the US economy added 559,000 new jobs in May alone, dropping the unemployment rate to 5.8%.

This means Biden's administration has now created over 2 million jobs since he took office - the most jobs created in the first four months of any presidency since records began in 1939.

Making the announcement during a Press address on Friday, June 4, Biden also noted that wages had also gone up for American workers.

"More jobs than have ever been created in the first four months of any presidency in modern history, triple the rate of my predecessor, eight times the rate of President Reagan," said Biden.

Biden said long-term unemployment also dropped by the second-largest amount ever recorded. Not only that, but the signs of further progress are already here.

"The unemployment rate is now below 6% for the first time since the pandemic hit," he said"The first time in 14 months, we saw the largest decline in the number of long-term unemployed, more — in more than an entire decade," he added.

The head of state further noted that 21 million more adults have gotten vaccinated, making it easy for them to return to work safely, terming its historical progress.

According to him, this was a testament to the new strategy of growing the US economy from the bottom up and the middle out under his leadership.

Biden credited this and other success achieved since he took office to the cooperation of the American people in responding to his effort to get COVID-19 under control.

Before Biden took office in January 2021, the US economy was in a tailspin, job growth had stalled, and COVID-19 was raging.

The average initial claims for unemployment insurance were over 830,000 per week. Now those claims have fallen below 430,000 — about half of what they were when he took office.
