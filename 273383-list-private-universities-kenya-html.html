List of private universities in Kenya in 2021- Updated.

There are over 30 private universities in Kenya which work to enhance the provision of quality education to the students of Kenya. Moreover, Kenya has shown a huge growth in the education sector from the last two decades. This article will provide you detailed information about the most popular private universities in Kenya.

Education is among the primary contributors in the growth and development of any nation and the whole world in general. In the year 2003, the government of Kenya introduced free primary education in the country. With this decision of the Kenyan government, children in Kenya are now able to access free education at the primary school level.

The Kenyan government made a leap and introduced free day secondary education.As such, students in day secondary schools are only required to pay for food and school uniforms. Those in boarding schools have had their fees significantly reduced. Higher education in Kenya is also improving, with the government keen on improving and promoting admission into technical and vocational training institutions.

The National Education Sector Plan of Kenya

The goals of National Education Sector Plan of Kenya include:

  • To provide proper administration for the education structure in order to provide necessary education facilities for all the children of Kenya.
  • To create a proper schooling system in order to deliver technical, academic, and professional needs for every student in Kenya.
  • Creation of a unified framework for the basic education of Kenya in order to enable productivity, achievability, and creativity.
  • An education structure for tertiary education in order to expand the pathway of learning for the young generation.
